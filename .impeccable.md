# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Casual Tamil Nadu voters — mobile-first, checking their constituency, candidates, and historical results during election season. Quick lookups dominate; they're not doing deep research. Secondary power users (researchers, journalists) exist but are not the primary audience. The product must be scannable and immediately legible to someone who is mildly curious, possibly on a slow mobile connection.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Political, Energetic**

This is a civic tool with attitude. It's not a government form — it's a dynamic data experience that reflects the energy of Tamil Nadu's vibrant democratic process. The design should feel like good political journalism: authoritative but accessible, data-driven but not cold. The interface should feel *alive*, especially during election season.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference**: NYT / Washington Post election maps — editorial clarity, authoritative cartographic style, readable under pressure. Data takes center stage; chrome is minimal and purposeful.

**Anti-references**: Avoid:
- Generic SaaS dark mode (cyan + purple glow, glassmorphism)
- Cluttered government portals (ECI, state websites)
- Sterile data dashboards (too clinical, too neutral)

**Visual tone**: Think editorial meets interactive — ink-on-paper authority fused with responsive, living data. The party colors (DMK red, AIADMK green, BJP orange, INC blue) ARE the palette; accent colors should support them, not compete. Dark theme is default and primary.

**Key aesthetic constraint**: The map is the hero — all chrome should defer to it. The overlay, filters, and header should feel like editorial annotations, not software UI.

### Design Principles

1. **Map First** — Every design decision should defer to the map. UI elements should feel like editorial overlays, not software panels.

2. **Party Colors as Identity** — DMK red, AIADMK green, BJP orange, INC blue are the visual language. Don't let brand accents (indigo/purple) compete. Reserve accents for non-political UI chrome.

3. **Mobile-First Legibility** — Primary users are on phones. Touch targets, font sizes, and information hierarchy must work at arm's length on a 375px screen.

4. **Bold Typography over Decoration** — Use type weight, size contrast, and hierarchy to create visual energy. Avoid decorative elements that don't carry information. The political context is dramatic enough — let the data speak.

5. **Energetic but Trustworthy** — Motion, color, and layout should feel alive and engaged without feeling biased or garish. Think *Al Jazeera English* or *BBC News* infographics — bold, credible, exciting.
